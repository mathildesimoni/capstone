	<tr><td colspan="2">ARI is an interdisciplinary peer-reviewed journal for physical and engineering sciences with main emphasis on overlapping topics to related fields. Emphasis is laid on original research articles covering: - models for interdisciplinary phenomena - mathematical or numerical analysis of models - experimental results on the validity of such models ARI also publishes: - news concerning relevant symposia workshops and other scientific meetings - book reviews covering interdisciplinary areas ARI encourages the publication of articles on multidisciplinary problems and methods: - pure mathematics - intended to lead to a better understanding of - physical phenomena - analysis of physical events for practical applications - modeling with firm physical foundations - approximations with rational bases and experiments testing a clearly defined - objective. Continuum physics materials science system science and Earth science are the main domains most capable of generating fertile intersections in which the journal should play a catalytic role. Review articles that may help to arouse the interest of scientists with diverse backgrounds in any interdisciplinary field are welcome but authors of review articles should contact one of the Editors or the Editor-in-Chief before submitting their paper.</td></tr>
